The Game

A trivia game with India at its core

All India Quiz was a single-player trivia game about India, spanning its history, geography, culture, cinema, and sport. The content was organised into twenty themed sets of twenty questions each, four hundred in total, so players always knew exactly what they were taking on and how far they had to go.

It was a One Eyed Octopus title for Android and iOS, and it struck a chord. At launch it landed in the top ten most downloaded games, which for a small studio making a homegrown quiz was a genuinely proud moment.

"A good trivia game is simple to pick up and surprisingly hard to put down. That balance was the whole job."

My Role

Producer, and the person watching over the design

On All India Quiz I was the producer. The detailed design was handled by a junior designer on the team, and my job was to oversee that design, keep the scope realistic, protect the quality bar, and steer the whole thing to a clean launch on both Android and iOS.

A lot of that work was mentoring. I helped shape the question structure, pushed on fairness and difficulty, reviewed the build as it came together, and made the calls that kept us on track. It is some of the work I am most proud of, because the result was someone else growing as a designer while we shipped a game that players genuinely took to.

03

The Design We Shipped

Simple on the surface, carefully tuned underneath

The hard part of any trivia game isn't writing questions. It's everything around them: how the content is organised, how difficulty feels across a set, and how scoring stays fair. These were the areas I kept the closest eye on as the design came together.

Question sets and content structure

Four hundred questions, grouped into twenty themed sets of twenty. That structure gave the game a clear shape: each set was a satisfying, self-contained run, and the spread of topics meant there was something for every kind of player, from history buffs to Bollywood fans.

Difficulty and pacing within a set

We paid real attention to how a set of twenty felt from start to finish, so it ramped fairly and never opened with a brick wall or coasted to the end. A lot of my oversight went into making sure the curve felt right rather than arbitrary.

Scoring and fairness

Scoring rewarded both accuracy and a bit of speed, without punishing a thoughtful player too harshly. The guiding rule throughout was fairness: clean questions, honest difficulty, and a score players could trust.

The Launch

Top ten most downloaded

All India Quiz launched on Android and iOS and quickly climbed into the top ten most downloaded games. For a small studio shipping a homegrown quiz, that was real validation: the idea connected, the content resonated, and the experience held together well enough that people kept playing and kept telling others.

Getting there came down to the producer fundamentals: a clear scope, a steady quality bar, and a junior designer I could back to do strong work. That is the kind of result I want on every team I join.
